## Candidate F Rule

For each split seed, Candidate F uses only train/support statistics:

1. Prepare positive-only NN support with `K = 40`.
2. Prepare classifier-probability weighted BC using the same classifier and
   weighted-demo recipe as the v0.1/v0.2 final matrix.
3. For the demos selected by positive-only NN support, read their classifier
   probabilities from the weighted-BC demo weights.
4. Compute:

```text
tail_threshold = 0.5 * unlabeled_prob_mean
below_count = count(p_selected < tail_threshold)
```

5. Select the branch:

```text
if below_count > 0:
    use weighted_bc
else:
    use candidate_e_gate
```

Candidate E gate:

```text
if initial positive-anchor action distance to labeled positive support > 3.0:
    execute weighted_bc for the whole episode
else:
    execute positive_only_nn for the whole episode
```

All stochastic policies in the router evaluator must use isolated per-policy RNG
streams, and all loaded RNN policies must be advanced consistently at every
timestep.

## Claim Gate

Candidate F can be promoted from scoped discovery to fresh Can-only validation
only if all of the following hold over the five frozen validation seeds:

- Candidate F pooled success is greater than the best completed non-oracle
  baseline per split.
- Candidate F is no worse than the best completed non-oracle baseline on at
  least `4/5` split seeds.
- Candidate F improves pooled success over positive-only NN and weighted BC.
- The paired/bootstrap uncertainty report is included and the text avoids a
  formal significance claim unless intervals support it.

If these gates fail or are neutral, report the result as a validation failure or
neutral extension and keep Candidate F scoped to discovery evidence.
